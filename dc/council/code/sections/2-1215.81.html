§ 2–1215.81. BID public space maintenance agreements.

*NOTE: This section was created by temporary legislation that will expire on August 5, 2021.*

*NOTE: This section includes amendments by temporary legislation that will expire on October 27, 2021.*

(a) Notwithstanding Chapter 3A of this title, and § 9-401.06(c), the Mayor may enter into an agreement, excluding grant agreements, with a BID corporation, as defined in § 2-1215.02(4), to maintain or improve a District-owned asset or public space including sidewalks, streets, parks, plazas, signage, and public art, within or partially within the boundaries of the BID, as defined in § 2-1215.02(7).

(b) The Mayor shall pay or reimburse to a BID corporation the reasonably incurred expenses for maintaining or improving a District-owned asset or public space or for engaging in planning activity under this section for services customarily provided by the District to any similar geographic area pursuant to § 2-1215.20(a).

(c) An agreement with a BID corporation made pursuant to this section to complete work on a District-owned asset or in public space shall contain provisions requiring adequate insurance and indemnification, and such work on a District-owned asset or in public space shall be subject to applicable permits.

(d) An agreement with a BID corporation made pursuant to this section shall not exceed $800,000 for a single fiscal year; provided, that a multiyear agreement shall be allowed, subject to annual appropriation.

(e) This section shall apply during a period of time for which the Mayor has declared a public health emergency pursuant to § 7-2304.01 and for 30 days thereafter.


(Dec. 23, 2020, D.C. Law 23-175, § 2, 67 DCR 13238; Mar. 16, 2021, D.C. Law 23-262, § 2, 68 DCR 001186.)
